About Me

Growing up as a homeschooler whose parents encouraged creative pursuits, I have been creating art for well over a decade. I've tried charcoal drawing, poetry contests, photography, and some attempts at painting that really make me appreciate Bob Ross's talent. I developed a particular love for digital art in my teenage years, which is when I started turning my hobbies into professional ventures. By sophomore year in college, I knew I loved to create but I didn't necessarily want to major in that field. Instead, I chose application development and found that front-end development bridges logic and creativity in a method complementary to my interests and skills.

Since graduating, I've been building my knowledge base of HTML, CSS, Bootstrap, Swiper and the like. I'm enrolled in a website development course on Udemy and have become friends with Stack Overflow, YouTube tutorials, and various documentation sites. I still have a lot to learn, but I'm grateful I've found a career where I get to create art in a way that's challenging, rewarding, and incorporates my analytical as well as creative sides.
